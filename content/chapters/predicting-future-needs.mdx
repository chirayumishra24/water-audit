### The Four Steps of Water Demand Forecasting

1. **Identify the Population:** Figure out the total number of residents. (e.g., 5 people per home in a 100-home society = 500 people).

2. **Apply Prescribed Standards:** Use established benchmarks:
   - **Urban Housing:** 135 Liters per person/day
   - **Rural Localities:** 55 Liters per person/day

3. **Perform the Calculation:** Total Daily Demand = Total Population × Standard Usage per Person.

4. **Factor in Additional Uses:** Add estimates for collective needs like landscaping, vehicle washing, and shared facilities such as mess halls or laundry areas.

### Infrastructure Planning from Demand Models

- **Storage Capacity:** Calculate the volume needed for overhead or underground tanks to hold at least one full day's supply.
- **Supply Requirements:** Alert the local municipal water authority about the expected daily demand so they can ensure the grid can handle the load.
- **Sustainability Planning:** Predict the generated greywater and plan recycling systems (like reed beds) right from the start to reduce fresh water demand.
