## Not all meters tell the same story

A common mistake is to treat every meter reading as interchangeable. In practice, each meter sits at a different point in the system and answers a different question.

Examples:

- the billing meter tells you what the utility sees
- the line meter tells you what passes into a branch
- the process meter tells you what one operation consumes

The audit needs all three perspectives, but it should never confuse them.

## First checks before analysis

Before interpreting a trend, confirm:

- unit of measure
- reading interval
- reset behavior
- calibration status if known
- whether the value is cumulative or interval-based

Without those checks, trend comparisons can become misleading very quickly.

## Patterns worth noticing

### Elevated base flow

If demand remains high during shutdown or non-production hours, the system may have continuous leakage, uncontrolled make-up flow, or cleaning practices outside planned hours.

### Step changes

Sudden level shifts may indicate process changes, a broken valve, a new connection, or a meter issue.

### Repeating spikes

Recurring spikes often correspond to cleaning cycles, batch production, irrigation events, or automatic control behavior.

## Meter hierarchy

Use the meter network like a hierarchy:

1. compare total supply meter against sum of sub-meters
2. identify where the largest unexplained difference appears
3. focus field observation on that branch or subsystem

This sequence makes later walkthrough work faster and more targeted.

## When data quality is poor

Sometimes the meters do not support a clean numerical answer. That does not stop the audit; it changes the confidence level.

The report should then distinguish:

- measured values
- estimated values
- inferred values

That distinction helps decision-makers know where to invest in better metering and where action can already begin.

## Bridge to field inspection

Meter interpretation is strongest when paired with direct observation. If the data suggests continuous flow during idle periods, the next move is not to guess. The next move is to inspect the physical system, which is the focus of [the site walkthrough chapter](/modules/measurement/site-walkthrough-and-loss-points/).
