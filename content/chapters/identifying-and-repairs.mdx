### Overview

<h3 className="text-2xl font-bold text-blue-900 mb-6">Identifying and Measuring Wastage</h3>

<Conversation>

<Message speaker="Mr. Sharma">

The first step is identifying wastage. Besides dripping taps, look for wet patches on walls or the ground—these indicate hidden pipe leaks.
</Message>

<Message speaker="Kavya">
I usually just look for dripping taps. Is there more to it?
</Message>

<Message speaker="Mr. Sharma">
Yes! Check overhead tanks too. Faulty ball valves often cause them to overflow, wasting thousands of liters.
</Message>

<Message speaker="Rohan">
Once we find a leak, how do we measure the exact loss?
</Message>

<Message speaker="Mr. Sharma">
Place a bucket under the leak, time it for one minute, and measure the volume. Multiply by 24 hours to see the daily loss.
</Message>

</Conversation>
