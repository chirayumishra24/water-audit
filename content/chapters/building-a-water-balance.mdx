### The "Source to Tap" Map

Understanding where your water comes from is the final piece of the puzzle. Moving from "how much I use" to "where it starts" connects your household habits to the larger ecosystem. Engaging with local water supply officials or visiting a treatment plant can turn a utility bill into a story of engineering and environment.

Before conducting an interview, it helps to understand the typical infrastructure. Most communities rely on a mix of surface water and groundwater.

- **Primary Sources:** Reservoirs (dams), rivers, or underground aquifers.
- **Storage:** Water towers or high-level tanks that use gravity to maintain pressure in your pipes.
- Water received from rains comes to us through natural as well as manmade water sources—rivers, wells, borewells, tanks, etc. It gets stored in big and small dams. Water from dams or storage tanks comes to us through canals, pipelines, etc. Knowing where your water comes to you and how much population is dependent on this water is necessary for your audit.

### Activity: Comparison and Analysis

In the water audit project, you will compare the average consumption data you collected first-hand (primary data) against prescribed consumption standards provided by government initiatives like the Jal Jeevan Mission.

### Comparison Standards for Water Usage

To analyze your findings, use the following prescribed standards as a benchmark for daily water use per person:

- **Rural Areas:** 55 Liters (L) per day.
- **Urban Areas:** 135 Liters (L) per day.

The above information will help you understand rainwater availability, usage and scarcity in your geography. Calculating water availability based on a district's geography and rainfall may not give you an accurate picture. All rainwater flows into rivers. The entire population, from the origin of the river up to the point where the river meets the sea, depends on river water. Some of these areas have high rainfall, and some have less rainfall. However, the water in the river basin belongs to the entire population.
