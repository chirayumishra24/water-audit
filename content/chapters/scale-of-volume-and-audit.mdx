The Ministry of Jal Shakti outlines a strict, six-step process to ensure no drop goes unaccounted for.

### The Six-Step Audit Process
1.  **Preliminary Survey:** Review bills and map layouts.
2.  **Measuring and Mapping:** Establish the water balance.
3.  **Detailed Technical Analysis:** Hunt for apparent and real losses.
4.  **Identifying Improvement Opportunities:** Look for conservation ways.
5.  **Action-Oriented Reporting:** Submit findings every two years.
6.  **Implementation and Monitoring:** Execute the strategy and monitor in real-time.

### The "Bucket and Stopwatch" Method
This is the most reliable way to measure flow rate at home.

  <p className="text-lg opacity-90 mb-4">Flow Rate (L/min) = (Volume in Liters / Time in Seconds) × 60</p>

### Efficiency Benchmarks
| Fixture Type | Efficient Flow (Eco) | High Flow (Wasteful) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Bathroom Tap | 4 – 6 L/min | > 10 L/min |
| Kitchen Tap | 6 – 9 L/min | > 12 L/min |
