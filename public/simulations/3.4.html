Rainwater Availability in Your Area (Secondary Data Activity)

Use reliable secondary data (rainfall records, area size, population) to estimate how much rainwater falls in your district/town/village/ward. This shows how much water nature provides — and how fairly it can be shared among people.

Key Goal: Calculate total rainwater → domestic share → per person availability. Compare to daily needs (55 L rural / 135 L urban).

What This Tells Us

Calculating rainwater availability based only on your district may not give the full picture — rivers carry water across regions. But this exercise helps you understand:

  • How much rain actually falls in your area
  • How much could be available for people (after agriculture/industry share)
  • Whether nature’s supply matches daily needs (55 L rural / 135 L urban)
  • Why saving water matters — especially in low-rainfall or high-population zones
