How a Water Audit is Conducted

A water audit follows a clear, step-by-step process — backed by the Ministry of Jal Shakti guidelines in India. Every drop is tracked so we can find waste and save water.

Follow these steps to conduct a complete water audit

1. Preliminary Survey

Starts with collecting old water bills, past usage patterns, and site visits. Map water sources, pipes, tanks, pumps, and discharge points. Sketch the layout to see how water moves. Check rules (CGWA, state permissions) so everything is legal and ready.

2. Measuring and Mapping Usage

Install or check flow meters at borewells, process lines, cooling towers, and outlets. Record water use over days/weeks to see changes (shifts, seasons). Create a full water balance: input vs output → find leaks, evaporation, or hidden use. Check water quality for reuse ideas.

3. Detailed Technical Analysis

Inspect pipes (ultrasonic sensors, pressure tests), classify losses:

  • Apparent losses: meter mistakes, theft, unrecorded use
  • Real losses: actual leaks, evaporation, inefficiency

Compare with industry benchmarks and ISO 14046 (water footprint). Find biggest problem areas for maximum savings.

4. Identifying Improvement Opportunities

Suggest fixes: reuse wastewater, change processes, install efficient tech. Example: cooling tower water → use for cleaning or dust control. Do simple cost-benefit check to choose best ideas. Match recommendations to rules and business goals.

5. Action-Oriented Reporting

Write a clear report: findings, recommendations, timelines, costs/savings. If using >100 m³ groundwater/day → submit to CGWA every 2 years. The report becomes your plan to track progress and make smart investments.

6. Implementation and Monitoring

Put the plan into action: fix leaks, upgrade fixtures, reuse systems, train staff. Use smart meters (IoT) for real-time tracking and alerts. Review regularly to keep improvements going. Use dashboards or Water Performance Indicators (WPIs) for ESG reporting.

Measuring Flow Rates

Flow rate = how much water comes out in a minute (Liters per Minute – L/min). Knowing this helps spot wasteful taps/showers.

The "Bucket and Stopwatch" Method

Tools Needed

  • Bucket with known volume (5L or 10L best)
  • Stopwatch (phone timer)
  • Calculator

Steps

  1. Place empty bucket under tap/shower
  2. Turn water on full → start timer
  3. Stop timer when bucket reaches known mark (e.g. 5L)
  4. Calculate: Flow rate (L/min) = Volume (L) ÷ Time (sec) × 60

Example: 5 liters fills in 20 seconds
→ 5 ÷ 20 = 0.25 L/sec
→ 0.25 × 60 = 15 L/min

Efficiency Benchmarks

Fixture Type Efficient Flow (Eco) High Flow (Wasteful)
Bathroom Tap 4 – 6 L/min > 10 L/min
Kitchen Tap 6 – 9 L/min > 12 L/min
Showerhead 7 – 9 L/min > 15 L/min

Pro Tip: If your shower >15 L/min, add a low-flow aerator → save up to 40% on water heating bills without losing pressure!
