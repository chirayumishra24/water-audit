Look Ahead — Plan for Tomorrow’s Water

In this final chapter, you’ll use all your audit data to predict future water needs. Just like weather forecasts use past patterns, you can model demand for new homes, hostels, or societies — helping communities plan storage, supply, and sustainability before shortages hit.

The Role of Data in Prediction

Your measurements (flow rates, daily use, wastage, greywater potential) become powerful tools for forecasting. Accurate data lets you answer: “What will happen if 500 more people move in next year?”

Good data = smart planning = no water crises tomorrow.

Steps for Modeling Future Water Scenarios

Example: Predicting demand for a new 100-home housing society or college hostel. Fill in your calculations below. Data is auto-saved and can be downloaded as PDF.

  1. Identify the Population
    Estimate total users (e.g., 5 people/home × 100 homes = 500 people).
  2. Apply Prescribed Standards
    Use benchmarks: Urban 135 L/person/day | Rural 55 L/person/day
  3. Perform the Calculation
    Total Daily Demand = Population × Standard Usage per Person
  4. Factor in Additional Uses
    Add collective needs (gardening, vehicle washing, shared facilities)

Predicting Infrastructure Needs

Use your predictions to plan ahead:

  • Storage Capacity: Size overhead/underground tanks to hold at least 1 full day’s supply (e.g., 67,500 L = 67.5 m³ tank minimum).
  • Supply Requirements: Inform municipal corporation about expected daily demand so the grid can cope.
  • Sustainability Planning: Estimate greywater generation and plan recycling (e.g., reed beds) to reduce net freshwater need.

Spreadsheets or simple programs can automate these calculations and show “what-if” scenarios.
