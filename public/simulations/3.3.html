Compare Your Data to National Standards

Now that you have your own primary data (from family surveys) and some secondary data (rainfall, population, availability), compare them to the official benchmarks set by the Government of India through the Jal Jeevan Mission.

  • Rural Areas: 55 Liters per person per day
  • Urban Areas: 135 Liters per person per day

Goal: See if your family’s use is below, at, or above these targets — and understand if your area’s supply can meet everyone’s needs.

Key Considerations for Analysis

  • Reliability: When using secondary data (standards, rainfall, population), always mention the source and year.
    Example: “The Jal Jeevan Mission aims for 55 L per person per day in rural areas (Press Information Bureau, Govt of India, 14 Aug 2024)”
  • Identifying High Consumption: Look for families or areas using more than average or prescribed limits. Reasons might include lifestyle, large gardens, leaks, or many guests.
  • Water Scarcity Check: Compare available water (rainfall + supply) to the number of people. Is there enough for 55 L or 135 L per person every day?

Always cite sources: Website name, date, link (or book/report title, publisher, year). This makes your work trustworthy and lets others check your information.

Finding Reliable Secondary Data

Search libraries, newspapers, magazines, or online using these keywords (add your district name for best results):

  • Rainfall + Year + name of district
  • Population + name of the district + census
  • Water availability per person + name of the district
  • Ministry of Statistics + Govt of India + Water
  • NITI Aayog + water report

Reliable sources include: Jal Jeevan Mission website, Press Information Bureau (pib.gov.in), NITI Aayog reports, Census of India, India Meteorological Department (IMD), Ministry of Jal Shakti.
