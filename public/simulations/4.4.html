Turn Waste Water into a Resource

Greywater is the gently used water from sinks, baths, washing machines, and kitchens (not from toilets). Instead of sending it down the drain, we can reuse it safely for plants, cleaning, toilet flushing, or groundwater recharge — reducing demand for fresh water.

Methods to Recycle & Reuse Greywater

Simple, low-cost ways to reuse greywater at home or school — no fancy system needed for basic uses.

  • Kitchen & Cooking Water
    Leftover water from washing vegetables or cooking (not too soapy) can directly water kitchen garden plants.
  • Hand Pump or Sink Runoff
    If not heavily soapy, direct runoff from hand pumps or sinks toward garden beds.
  • Toilet Flushing
    Collected greywater can be used for flushing toilets (research "DIY greywater toilet systems" for safe setups).
  • Natural Filtration: Reed Bed System
    Schools/communities can build a simple reed bed:
    1. Sedimentation tank — particles settle
    2. Reed/plants (e.g. Canna indica, Colocasia) roots absorb pollutants & heavy metals
