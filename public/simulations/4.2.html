Fix It Yourself — Stop the Drip!

Most leaking taps don’t need a plumber — just a little attention. Start with simple fixes like tightening or replacing a worn washer. With basic tools and adult supervision, you can stop wasting water and learn real skills.

Step-by-Step: Fixing a Leaking Tap

Watch these videos first, then follow the steps carefully (with adult help).

  1. Preparation
    Turn off the water mains (main valve) to stop water flow while working.
  2. Disassembly – Remove Handle & Cover
    Use a spanner to remove the tap cover (often under hot/cold sign). Unscrew the handle.
  3. Remove Bonnet & Headgear
    Unscrew the bonnet completely. You’ll see the body washer, O-ring, and jumper valve (it may fall out).
  4. Replace Worn Parts
    Replace the old body washer, O-ring, and jumper valve with new ones from your kit.
  5. Lubricate & Reassemble
    Apply tap lubricant to the spindle and valve. Refit bonnet, spindle, and handle — don’t over-tighten.
  6. Final Check
    Turn water back on. Test the tap — no drip? Great job! You just saved hundreds of liters.

Essential Tools & Safety First

Tools & Materials

  • Wrench / spanner
  • Plumber tape
  • New body washer, O-ring, jumper valve
  • Tap lubricant

Safety Rules

  • Always turn off water mains first
  • Use tools carefully — adult supervision required
  • Avoid slippery areas
  • Never work alone on plumbing tasks

Pro Tip: Invite a local plumber to your school once — they can show the tap mechanism live and teach safe tool use!
