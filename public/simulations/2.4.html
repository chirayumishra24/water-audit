From Source to Tap — Connect the Dots

Knowing where your water comes from connects your daily use to the bigger picture — rivers, dams, wells, treatment plants, and community systems. Talking to local officials turns your water bill into a real story of nature and engineering.

The “Source to Tap” Map

Most places get water from a mix of natural and man-made sources. Understanding this helps you see how your use affects the whole system.

  • Primary Sources: Reservoirs (dams), rivers, lakes, or underground aquifers (borewells/wells)
  • Storage: Big tanks, water towers, or overhead tanks that use gravity to push water through pipes
  • Journey: Rain → rivers/dams → canals/pipelines → treatment → storage → your home tap

Key Question: How many people depend on the same water source as your home? This shows why saving water matters for the whole community!

Conducting the Interview: Key Questions

Visit your Gram Panchayat office, Municipal Corporation, Ward Office, or local water supply department. Ask politely — officials love sharing knowledge with curious students!
Tip: Write your answers below. Everything is auto-saved and can be downloaded as PDF.

1. What is the source of water for our locality?

2. How much water does each household receive per day? What is the amount allotted per person per day?

3. Do you face any challenges in supplying sufficient water to all? What are those challenges?

4. How does rainwater contribute to water availability in your area? What efforts are made to store rainwater?

5. Are there any seasonal changes in water availability in the locality?

6. Any suggestions for better water management by individuals and families?

7. Can you help create a simple map showing water sources (wells, tanks, delivery points) in our neighborhood?
