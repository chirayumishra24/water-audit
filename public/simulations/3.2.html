From Daily Numbers to Meaningful Averages

Water use changes day to day — long shower Monday, no garden watering Tuesday. Averages smooth out the ups and downs so you can see your typical use, predict bills, detect leaks, and set realistic saving goals for the whole family.

Goal: Know exactly how much water your home really uses on an average day — and per person!

1. Calculating the Daily Household Average

Collect data for at least 7 days (to capture weekends vs weekdays). Then use this simple formula:

Example

  • Total water used over 7 days = 1,050 Liters
  • 1,050 ÷ 7 = 150 L/day for the whole house

2. Calculating Consumption Per Person (Per Capita)

This is the most useful number — it lets you compare your family to national or global benchmarks, no matter the household size.

Example

House uses 150 L/day and 3 people live there:
150 ÷ 3 = 50 Liters per person per day

Compare: Many Indian rural areas aim for 55 L/person/day (Jal Jeevan Mission standard). Urban use is often higher.

3. Estimating Monthly and Yearly Totals

Bills come monthly or quarterly — convert your daily average to bigger units (like cubic meters m³) to check against your actual bill.

Timeframe Calculation Example (at 150 L/day)
Weekly Daily Avg × 7 1,050 L
Monthly Daily Avg × 30 4,500 L or 4.5 m³
Yearly Daily Avg × 365 54,750 L (or 54.75 m³)

1 m³ = 1,000 Liters — so your yearly use might be around 50–60 m³ per household!

Why These Averages Matter

  • Detecting Leaks: If your calculated average is 5 m³/month but the bill says 15 m³ → silent leak (toilet, pipe) wasting water 24/7.
  • Capacity Planning: If your area cuts supply to 100 L/person/day but your average is 150 L → you need to reduce by 50 L/person (e.g., shorter showers by 2 min each).
